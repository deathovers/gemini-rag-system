# DocuChat AI User Guide

Welcome to **DocuChat AI**! This guide will help you get the most out of your document assistant.

## 🌟 What is DocuChat AI?
DocuChat AI is a tool that lets you "talk" to your PDF documents. Instead of scrolling through hundreds of pages, you can simply ask questions and get immediate answers with citations.

## 🚀 Getting Started

### 1. Uploading Documents
- Click the **Upload** button.
- Select one or more PDF files (e.g., manuals, contracts, research papers).
- Wait for the "Processing Complete" notification. This means the AI has read and indexed your files.

### 2. Asking Questions
- Type your question in the chat box.
- Be specific! Instead of "What does this say?", try "What are the payment terms mentioned in the agreement?"
- The AI will search through all uploaded documents to find the answer.

### 3. Reading Citations
- Every answer provided by the AI includes **Sources**.
- You will see the filename and the page number where the information was found.
- **Tip:** Always verify the AI's answer by checking the cited page in your original document.

## ⚠️ Important Rules & Limitations

### Grounding (The "Truth" Rule)
The AI is strictly limited to the documents you upload. 
- If you ask a question that isn't covered in the PDFs (e.g., "What is the weather today?"), the AI will respond: *"The answer was not found in the uploaded documents."*
- This prevents the AI from making up facts (hallucinating).

### Session Privacy
Your documents are tied to your current session. If you refresh the page or clear your session, you will need to re-upload your documents to start a new conversation.

### File Types
Currently, only **PDF** files are supported. Ensure your PDFs are not password-protected or encrypted.

## 💡 Tips for Better Results
- **Clear Text:** Ensure your PDFs have selectable text (not just scanned images without OCR).
- **Context:** If you have multiple documents, mention which one you are interested in (e.g., "According to the 'Employee Handbook', what is the vacation policy?").
